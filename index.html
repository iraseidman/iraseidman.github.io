Analysis Academy's Philosophy

It's tough to find a school these days that meets all the criteria that it should - educates every child to their fullest potential, prepares students for their paths as adults, and creates well-rounded people/citizens all while creating community and keeping everyone safe and connected.

The Analysis Academy is the answer to all of these issues - a small school in New York City that feeds off of students' natural curiosity to make a home for intellectual, social, physical, and emotional for all of our students. Founded by teachers and parents rather than technologists, our philosophy is:

  1. Children are free as inspired by A.S. Niell’s work at Summerhill - kids can choose what they want to take, when they show up, what they work on, etc… Depending on the student's choices we will just keep students and their families what type of diploma they will be on track to earn by graduation.

  2. Students can learn best on their own - every class is self-paced and run through videos, assignments, and exams through Khan Academy so kids can work asynchronously and at their own pace. Classes will meet on a schedule and if kids are ahead they can keep working independently or if they are behind they can work with a tutor until they are caught back up with the class.

  3. Teachers are tutors who teach ALL students how to analyze their own mistakes and learn from them - classes are not taught with a teacher in front of a room but rather kids working together or independently if they prefer and just meet with a teacher once a day if needed to help them with the skills they can not master on their own or in class.

  4. The pillars of our community are - love first, we are democratically run where every member of the school has the same vote, and curiosity for all.

  5. No bells, ever.
